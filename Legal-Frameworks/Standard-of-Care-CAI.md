# The Luevano Standard: Establishing a New Standard of Care for AI
    
    ### Overview
    This document outlines the 'Standard of Care' for high-stakes AI systems, as proposed by the Algorithmic Consistency Initiative (ACI). It transitions the industry from 'Black-Box' models to 'Glass-Box' transparency through the implementation of Constitutional AI.
    
    ### The Problem: Design Defects in AI
    Traditional AI models are often 'Black Boxes'—their decision-making process is opaque and they are prone to 'Behavioral Mimicry' of biased historical data. In legal terms, deploying such unconstrained models in high-stakes domains (hiring, credit, healthcare) where safer alternatives exist may be considered a 'Design Defect'.
    
    ### The Solution: The Luevano Standard
    The Luevano Standard mandates a 'Constitutional Design' that includes:
    1. **Explicit Normative Principles:** A written 'Constitution' derived from law and policy that governs model behavior.
    2. **Reasoning Traces:** Auditable logs that explain *why* an AI reached a specific conclusion or refusal.
    3. **Constitutional Error Rate (CER):** A quantifiable metric to measure how often an AI violates its own constitutional principles.
    
    ### Impact on Liability and Governance
    By adopting the Luevano Standard, organizations can:
    * Shift from reactive damage control to proactive 'Accountable Design'.
    * Align with the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0).
    * Establish a robust defense against claims of algorithmic discrimination and negligence.
